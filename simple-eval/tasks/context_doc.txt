INTERNAL MEMO — MERIDIAN ROBOTICS, INC.
From: Priya Natarajan, VP of Operations
To: All department leads
Date: April 14, 2026
Subject: Q1 2026 operations review

Team,

Q1 closed stronger than forecast. Revenue came in at $8.4 million, up 12% from
Q4, driven mostly by the fulfillment-robotics line. Our friends in sales will
share the full pipeline picture at the offsite, but the short version is that
the Kestrel Automation merger rumors did not slow our enterprise deals the way
some analysts predicted. Marketing's "Warehouse of Tomorrow" campaign, which
was supposed to launch in February, slipped to late Q2 — more on that from
Dara's team separately, but it did not appear to hurt the quarter.

Site performance. We now operate three fulfillment warehouses: Fresno (opened
2021), Tulsa (opened 2023), and Elkhart, which came online in January 2026.
Order volumes for the quarter: Fresno processed 41,200 orders, Tulsa processed
38,900, and Elkhart, still ramping, processed 12,750. Elkhart's ramp is
tracking about three weeks ahead of the plan we presented in the December
board deck, which is a credit to the launch team that relocated from Tulsa.

The one significant operational incident of the quarter happened on February
9, when a conveyor drive fault at Tulsa halted picking for 6 hours. Between
overtime, expedited freight to keep SLAs, and the repair itself, we estimate
the incident cost about $90,000. Root cause was a worn coupling that the
vibration sensors flagged but the alert routing misfiled; the fix for the
alert pipeline shipped in March.

Larkspur rollout. Our new warehouse management system, Larkspur, completed
its rollout at Fresno on March 3. Cutover weekend went smoothly — pick rates
were back to baseline within four days, versus the ten we had budgeted. Tulsa
is scheduled for July, and the Elkhart date is still to be determined, since
we want at least two quarters of stable baseline data there before we change
its tooling. A reminder that Larkspur training modules are live on the
learning portal; completion is mandatory for shift leads before their site
cuts over.

People. Headcount ended the quarter at 312, with 47 hires in Q1, concentrated
in Elkhart fulfillment and the controls-engineering group. Quarterly attrition
was 4.1%, roughly flat with last year. The cafeteria vendor change at Fresno
(from Grainhouse to Fieldstone Catering) generated more all-hands questions
than any other topic this quarter, which I choose to read as a sign that
nothing serious is broken.

Safety. Recordable incidents fell from 9 in Q4 to 5 this quarter. Three of
the five were hand injuries during tote handling, and the new tote-edge guards
going in at all sites in May are aimed squarely at that pattern. Zero
lost-time incidents for the second consecutive quarter.

Supply chain. We completed the switch of our battery-pack supplier from
Voltcore to Ashgrid in February, saving 8% per unit at equivalent cell specs.
Ashgrid's second-source agreement also gives us an option on the higher-density
packs for the AGV refresh next year. Chassis steel and harness components
remain on allocation, but our buffer stock policy kept builds unblocked all
quarter.

Priorities for Q2: land the Tulsa Larkspur cutover in July without SLA
impact, finish Elkhart's ramp to full two-shift operation, and get the
tote-edge guard retrofit done at all three sites in May.

Questions to me or your site director.

— Priya
